    Just Back From...Åhus, Sweden

    Trip Duration: 3 days

    Flight Plan: I flew directly from New York City to Copenhagen, and then drove about an hour and a half into Åhus. The direct flight put me across international seas with ease, and the short drive into the town of Åhus was a picturesque way to be welcomed to the Swedish countryside.

    Lauren Sheffield

    Where I Stayed: Gastis Hotel Åhus. Situated right along the water on the outskirts of Åhus, this was the perfect hotel to really get a feel for the quaint boating town. The property had a very homey B&B vibe, melding together antique elements with Swedish minimalistic flair. You could find tea and cookies placed on old-fashioned wood tables throughout the day, as well as head downstairs to the sitting room full of plush seating for cocktails served via a deco gilded bar cart.

    Courtesy Gastis Hotel

    Best Thing I Ate: Lunch at Kippers Restaurant in Kristianstad–a neighboring town about ten minutes away from Åhus with just as many winsome sites to see. After drinks outside on the patio, enjoying the gorgeous weather Sweden is known for during the summer, we went downstairs to a more medieval setting where we had a Swedish Smorgasboard style lunch. This meant that everything was served buffet style and you could test your palette on an array of traditional, small bites. Think charcuterie boards with manchego cheese and quince jelly, herring served with olive oil, vinegar, garlic and chile, or my personal favorite, 'Toast Skagen' made up of North Sea shrimp in dill & mayonnaise served on grilled toast and topped with caviar.

    Phil Barton

    Best Hang Out Spot: Our hotel had a great patio where we were able to sit along the water and take in the views of sailboats passing by. The town of Åhus is so peaceful, and it was nice to be able to to embrace the lackadaisical vibe during my stay. Curling up with a good book and taking in the stunning views here for an hour or two each day was idyllic, and enjoying a cocktail in front of the water view wasn't so bad either.

    Lauren Sheffield

    Best Daytime Activity: One day I visited the Absolut Elyx distillery, which helms the world's first true luxury vodka and is located right in the heart of Åhus. Here, I took a martini masterclass, learning all the ins and outs of how to make a martini whether it be shaken, stirred, dirty or garnished with a twist. This was such a fun way to test out different iterations on such a classic cocktail, as well as gain a rich history of vodka distilling itself. Here, they distill all of their vodka in massive copper stills, which resulted in a stellar, silky taste.

    Phil Barton

    Phil Barton

    Best Night Out: One night we travelled into the forest area of Åhus for dinner at The Blacksmith. The restaurant is set in a castle-like structure in the middle of the woods; the juxtaposition between an elegant dinner party and the rustic, natural setting was something out of a fairytale.

    Phil Barton

    Trip Highlight: Glass blowing. One day we visited a glass blowing studio right near our hotel, where I was able to make my own vermouth bottle. While the heat was definitely hard to take at times, it was fascinating to see how much actually goes into the craft and test my hand at something completely out of my comfort zone.

    Phil Barton

    Could not Leave Home Without: A simple jumper you can easily dress down for day and up for night, a great sun hat, a light bucket bag for keeping your essentials together during a full day of activities and Bobbi Brown lipstick in 'Blackberry' for a sultry punch to your evening looks.

    Courtesy

    Most Inspiring Aspect: While my trip was short, there was something about this quaint little boating town that instantly put me at ease. Whether I was walking by the little houses nestled up next to each other or traveling off the beaten path to the lakes and country parts, the town had an air of simpler times that you couldn't help but be affected by.

    Thomas Welch

    Why Åhus? With its proximity to Denmark and coastal Swedish vibes, this is the perfect little town to make a stop in whether you're in the mood for boating, a lesson in vodka or simply want to experience a place that boasts a quintessential Swedish summer.
