    Julie Walters urges women to be vigilant after breast cancer scare

    Actress Julie Walters is urging women to be vigilant about breast health after a cancer scare last year. The Educating Rita star admitted that she hadnt checked herself enough before she started to experience breast pain.

    26 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Actress Julie Walters is urging women to be vigilant about breast health after a cancer scare last year.

    The Educating Rita star admitted that she hadn’t checked herself enough before she started to experience breast pain.

    Luckily her discomfort was simply due to a blocked duct, but Julie, 65, says the experience has made her more self-aware.

    Actress Julie Walters, pictured at the Burberry Festive Film Premiere on November 3 last year, is urging women to be vigilant about breast health after a cancer scare last year

    ‘About six or seven months ago I had a bit of a pain in my breast and I was like, “Oh my God, here we go,” ’ said Julie, who was nominated for a Bafta earlier this month for her role in the film Brooklyn.

    ‘I went to get it checked out but it wasn’t anything sinister. The doctors said I should have a mammogram anyway. I hadn’t had one in about 30 years and I thought “It’s my fault” because I haven’t checked anything.’

    Julie, who trained as a nurse in Birmingham before turning to acting, made the admission at the unveiling of new 3D breast cancer equipment at the Royal Surrey County Hospital last week. 

    Their tomosynthesis machine, paid for by a fundraising appeal, works by using a curved X-ray motion to build a 3D picture of breast tissue. Studies have found the scan boosts early detection of breast cancer by 25 per cent.

    Julie urged other women to familiarise themselves with the signs of breast cancer, such as changes to the shape or size of the breast (stock image)

    Traditional mammograms offer only a two-dimensional view, meaning abnormalities can be masked, leading to a delayed diagnosis.

    Julie urged other women to familiarise themselves with the signs of breast cancer, such as changes to the shape or size of the breast. ‘Keep getting checked,’ she said.

    She also spoke of her sadness at the death of Harry Potter co-star Alan Rickman, from pancreatic cancer.

    ‘I can understand why he and other people don’t want to talk about [their illness] and they keep it private. They have enough to deal with it without making it public.’
