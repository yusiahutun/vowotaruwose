    26 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Tipping Point viewers were left in stitches by a contestant's answer to a politics-related question.

    The segment, taken from the episode on Thursday (September 30), was shared by a fan on Twitter, writing: "I just saw this on TV and I actually cannot stop laughing."

    In the clip, host Ben Shephard can be heard asking one contestant on the quiz show: "Which UK political party shares its name with a collective noun for 'moles'?"

    Karen, the woman in an orange top, pauses and says: "I'm not 100% certain, so I'm going to pass it to Alice I think."

    Ben then repeats the question to Alice, who answers: "For some reason Liberal Democrats is shouting out to me. I'm not sure why, I think I'm going for Liberal Democrats."

    Some viewers couldn’t help but notice Ben seemingly attempting to hide his own laughter at the contestant’s answer.

    Another joked it could be a word play, adding: "Liberal DeMOLEcrats."

    A third laughed: "Ed's speech was greeted with rapt attention."

    Some tried their luck and said: "I thought it might have been ‘a green’. They are known for being very industrious, tunnelling away."

    "Is it, a UKIP of moles?" another wrote and a third added: "I just thought, 'They tear up everything!' which sounds like Tories, in more than one way."

    The correct answer, as a Twitter user posted with a Wikipedia page, is Labour.

    It explains: "Male moles are called 'boars', females are called 'sows'. A group of moles is called a 'labour'."

    One commented that "Labour" is the only option that is "grammatical or generally makes sense".
