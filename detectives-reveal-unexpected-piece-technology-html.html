    24 May 2024, 00:00

    Detectives have revealed how a man who savagely beat a mother of two to death then dumped her body in the woods was snared with the help of a simple breathalyser test. 

    Divorcee Nicole White, from Orting, Washington, was reported missing on the night of her second date with Jonathan Harris, from Graham, whom she met on a dating app, on June 6 2015. 

    Harris denied any involvement in the disappearance of Ms White, but a chilling documentary reveals how a breathalyser fixed to his car as a result of a past DUI charge led police to remote woodland where her body was found wrapped in tarp. 

    The device, which Harris had to blow into in order to start his car, took a photograph at each use complete with a date and time stamp. Investigators matched trees in the background of a photo with a location pinged from Harris' phone at 6pm the night after the victim disappeared.

    There, police discovered his date's remains. The forensic anthropologist who carried out the autopsy says in Really Channel's Swipe Right for Murder how Harris' frenzied attack on Nicole was 'one of the worst cases I've ever seen'.   

    Jonathan Harris was found guilty of killing mother-of-two Nicole White after they met on an online dating site in Washington

    Harris had taken his picture at the forest where Nicole's body was discovered, this was to activate an ignition airlock due to a previous DUI charge

    When the Pierce Country Sheriff Department had enough evidence to secure a warrant and search Harris' home, bloodied clothes that matched what he was seen wearing in CCTV footage were covered in Nicole's blood. 

    Detective Sergeant John Delgato for the sheriff's department explains in the documentary how, after two weeks of extensive searching by the police, FBI officers and civilian volunteers through dense woodland they still had not found the missing mother.

    Meanwhile Harris had been taken in for questioning after it was discovered that he had been on a date with Nicole on June 6 2015.

    TIMELINE OF EVENTS 

    Spring 2015: Nicole White first spoke to Jonathan Harris on an online dating app

    May 2015: They go on their first date, with Nicole's friend joining them to play pool

    June 6 2015: Pair go on a second date

    June 7 2015: Nicole is reported missing when she doesn't return home to her two sons

    June 20 2015: After two weeks of searching her body is found in remote woodland 

    June 2015: Harris continued to deny involvement in the murder but key evidence was discovered by detectives to secure a second degree charge

    November 2015: Harris' earlier second degree murder charge changed to first degree murder after autopsy revealed he stomped Nicole to death

    June 23 2016: Harris is charged with Nicole's murder and sentenced to 26 years in prison

    He denied any involvement, claiming Nicole had driven him part of the way to his home before abandoning him at a local 24/7 garage.

    Facts from his statement didn't add up as CCTV footage revealed he hadn't been there at the time he had stated, and they were suspicious about Harris' explanation that he had walked for six miles until he got home.

    Determined to connect him to Nicole's disappearance Delgato went through every piece of evidence they had gathered.

    He spoke with a bouncer, and Harris' friend Matt Martino, who had seen the pair on the evening of their date.

    Martino described how they looked like they were having fun, but said Harris' mood had switched at several points after an ex-girlfriend appeared in the bar with a new partner.

    He recalled how he had seen Harris smash two beer bottles in a 'fit of rage and anger'.

    And later Martino, after Harris was found guilty of murder, said he knew about his temper: 'I've seen him get blackout angry before and I think sadly that is what happened with Nicole. 

    'Something pushed him over the edge. He snapped, he got black out angry and unfortunately she paid the price for it.' 

    Nicole's body was found in a remote area of woodland wrapped in a tarp and painter's canvas in the location from Harris' mobile phone and the photo.

    Detective Sergeant John Delgato for the Pierce County sheriff's department was determined to find Nicole's body and used the photo to match Harris' location to his mobile phone ping

    Nicole's sister Melissa Nieto explained in the documentary that she had taken care of her sister's sons since her death but hadn't been able to tell them what had happened

    Her family were left distraught after learning that she had suffered at the hands of Harris who had beaten her, drove her out to the woods, left her body and cleaned up his mess.

    The autopsy was carried out by forensic anthropologist Dr Katherine Taylor, who said in the documentary, that Harris' frenzied attack on Nicole was 'one of the worst cases I have ever seen'. 

    ‘One very possible explanation for that would be focal on the sternum, stomping comes to mind for that. I can’t say what instrument he might have used except there was substantial force used.'

    Harris was initially charged with second degree murder but after the autopsy results proved that he had stomped on her, the charge was upped to first degree murder.

    Along with blood found on his clothes and his carpet Harris was found guilty nearly a year to the day after her body was discovered and sentenced to 26 years in prison. 

    Nicole's sister Melissa Nieto took in her two young sons who she says have no idea what happened to their mother.

    'I haven’t told her kids anything about it, I just say she is in heaven… I think they know.'
