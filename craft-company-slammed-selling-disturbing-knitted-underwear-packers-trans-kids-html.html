    Craft Company slammed for selling 'disturbing' knitted underwear 'packers' for trans kids

    A craft company have been slammed for flogging 'disturbing' knitted 'packers' for trans kids to put in their underwear so they feel like they have male genitals - even allowing parents to pick their length.

    15 Sep 2024, 00:00

    A craft company have been slammed for flogging 'disturbing' knitted 'packers' for trans kids to put in their underwear so they feel like they have male genitals - even allowing parents to pick their length.

    US- based company Stitchbug Studio claimed they had made their mini genital-shaped Bitty Bug Soft Packer 'appropriate for children' since their other 'gender affirming' products are only fit for teens and adults.

    The controversial £4.20 item is available in multiple colours and sizes between 1in-2.5in and was even advertised on the company's website using a photo of a teddy bear with a bulge protruding from his children's underpants.

    When the listing was shared on Twitter on Sunday it faced a barrage of criticism branding it 'insane', while an outspoken UK trans campaigner known as Rose of Dawn labelled the idea 'absurd' and 'inappropriate'.

    Stitchbug Studio have been slammed for flogging 'disturbing' knitted 'packers' for trans kids to put in their underwear so they feel like they have male genitals - even allowing parents to pick their length. Pictured, Stitchbug Studios' Bitty Bug Soft Packer

    It was even advertised on the company's website using a photo of a teddy bear with a bulge protruding from his children's underpants (pictured)

    Trans campaigner Rose (pictured), from London, said she finds it 'disturbing' that this kind of thing is being 'normalised'

    Similar adult 'packers' have become popular in the trans community to help reduce gender dysphoria and anxiety, however this range were noticeably smaller than usual.

    Stitchbug Studio responded to the criticism by removing the image of the bear and adding a disclaimer in the product's description denying it's aimed at 'infants or very young children'.

    However, the controversial item's blurb does still state the item is in 'youth sizes', and 32-year-old Rose believes the denial was 'damage control' for the company's 'dodgy' product.

    Rose, from London, said: 'It's inappropriate for a child of this age. It's warped to even think this is acceptable. I find it quite disturbing that this kind of thing is being normalised. 

    The controversial £4.20 item is available in multiple colours and sizes between 1in-2.5in. Pictured, Stitchbug Studios' adult packers on their website

    One Twitter user posted screenshots of Stitchbug Studio's listing and it's since gained more than 2,000 likes, 2,400 retweets and 1,500 replies (pictured)

    'It's putting a message onto a very child that 'you're not okay with your genitals, put this little thing on instead'. I think there's a big risk that they can be affected mentally big this.

    'A small child isn't really aware of biological reality. A lot of kids aren't really aware or even think about what the other sex's genitals are like.'

    'Even if they are dealing with gender dysphoria, it's more the social side of things rather than genital discomfort.

    STITCHBUG STUDIO'S ORIGINAL DESCRIPTION FOR THEIR BITTY BUG SOFT PACKER IN FULL 

    'Bitty Bug soft packer is a custom prosthetic packer in sizes appropriate for children, made from soft durable yarn and polyester filling.

    'It is easy to wear pinned to undies or tucked in a packing pouch. Silicone prosthetics do not come in sizes appropriate for kiddos. The Bitty Bug soft packer is lightweight, comfortable, low maintenance, and can be tossed in the washing machine. Adorable AND functional!

    'Each Bitty Bug is handmade to order. We also make the Lil' Bug and Lil' Bug Jr. in sizes suitable for teens and adults. If you need a Bitty Bug in a different size or configuration, please reach out to us and we will make the necessary adjustments.'

    They later changed it and added:

    'NOTE TO OUR CUSTOMERS: This product is not for infants or very young children. There has been some confusion for those who are not aware of what some trans youths go through and how prosthetics can help their experience.

    'Thank you to the parents out there who support your trans youngsters, and we are honored to be a small part of their process.'

    'This is a grift. Stitchbug Studio know full well there's something dodgy behind this but the activist circles pat people on the back for doing such absurd things and they're trying to capitalise on it.

    'Five or ten years ago anyone would have thought it was some sort of skit from a comedy show but now it's the done thing. I don't quite understand how any normal person can see it as acceptable.

    'This is like a virtue-signalling stunt rather than actually caring about what a young person might be going through with gender dysphoria.'

    Rose believes US-based Stitchbug Studio's item is part of a bigger issue where children are skipping the lengthy and important process of transitioning.

    Rose said: 'With trans kids a lot of activism surrounds the idea you've got to affirm whatever your child says and if they don't they're going to kill themselves and I don't think that aligns with reality.

    'Trans kids are probably more rare than people make them out to be anyway, but now so much is on affirmation that all a little buy has to say is 'I'm a girl' and that's it.

    'Really there should be psychiatric care. It should be a long process making sure things are right, but so many people just want to cut all of that out.'

    One Twitter user posted screenshots of Stitchbug Studio's listing and it's since gained more than 2,000 likes, 2,400 retweets and 1,500 replies.

    They penned: 'The entire purpose of a prosthetic penises is if a trans-identified female has some sort of genital anxiety, they can wear it to alleviate that dysphoria.

    'WHY would a very small, diaper wearing child have any concerns, anxieties, or even *awareness* of their genitals?'

    A second commented: 'These things shouldn't even be designed, manufactured and then sold. Why is this permitted,' while a third added:

    'This sums up the whole insanity. Stop thinking about kids genitals and focus on making sure they get sleep, are fed, cuddled, read them a book occasionally, maybe teach them the alphabet... There is a very very dark seedy side to transing children and here is the evidence.'

    Elsewhere, a fourth wrote: 'Munchausen Syndrome by proxy.'

    Pictured, the packer's seller Stitchbug Studios' website which sells the controversial items

    Stitchbug Studio, who claimed they had made their mini genital-shaped Bitty Bug Soft Packer 'appropriate for children' since their other 'gender affirming' products are only fit for teens and adults, reacted to criticism of their product (pictured)

    The company revised their product description to include the new disclaimed that the packer's are not for infants or very young children (pictured)

    Another social media user wrote: 'A few years ago this would have been considered highly inappropriate and parents buying such things for their children would have been considered to be inappropriately sexualising their children. We are in the upside down.'

    Meanwhile, a further commented: 'God. Send the meteors. We're done here.'

    In a statement posted to Facebook, Stitchbug Studio hit back at 'trans-unaware people' for their criticism and sought to clarify their position.

    They said: 'Our packers are made for the trans community, and the people who support them.

    'We recently faced some pretty intense 'attention' from the Twittersphere when two trans-unaware people made some rather strange assumptions about our youth packers sizes, namely that they were intended for use on infants. (Like... what?)

    'We've revised the product description, and hopefully this clears it up for those who are confused. It will do nothing for those who are simply hateful and intent on attacking the trans community, but those folks aren't our audience and aren't worth any more time.

    Pictured, the new image on the Bitty Bug Soft Packer listing without the cuddly toy (pictured)

    Stitchbug Studio reacted to criticism of their product and commented: 'Packers are not for babies or infants, that would be pretty weird and pointless. Transphobes will be blocked' (pictured)

    'We will always ALWAYS support and serve our trans community, and the AMAZING parents out there doing their level best to support and love their trans youths and their needs. Y'all are our literal heros.'

    The studio later added: 'For those who don't know and genuinely want to, soft packers are for transgender and non-binary people who find that wearing a prosthetic reduces gender dysphoria. They are also sometimes used as anatomical models in educational settings.

    'We make a product that is an alternative to silicone prosthetics, as it is lighter, cooler to wear, easier to maintain, easier to make in a variety of sizes and colors and a lot less expensive.

    'Yes, we have been honored to make a few packers at the request of loving parents of trans youths (not babies FFS) who feel it would help with their dysphoria.

    'We do not interact with children at all, that would be weird and inappropriate. It's a beautiful and encouraging thing to know that there are kids out there growing up with parents who support, encourage and love them unconditionally.' 

    Stitchbug Studio were contacted for comment.
