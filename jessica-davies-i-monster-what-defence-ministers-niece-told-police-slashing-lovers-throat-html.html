    'I am a monster': What minister's niece told police after she'd knifed her lover to death

    By Peter Allen for MailOnline
    Created: 07:17 EST, 11 January 2010


    Drink problems: Jessica Davies arrives at court yesterday

    The niece of defence minister Quentin Davies stabbed her lover to death following a frenzied sex session and then told police: 'I am a monster'.

    Jessica Davies, 30, was covered in blood when officers found her cradling the naked body of Olivier Mugnier, 24, at her one-bedroomed Paris flat, a French court heard yesterday.

    Although barely able to stand because of the amount of drugs and drink she had consumed, the former model confessed to the crime, saying: 'I did it. I can't tell you why, but I did it. I am a monster'.

    Holding up a knife following the November 2007 attack, Davies added: 'I wanted to cut him a bit and it went right in.'

    Mr Mugnier had multiple stab wounds to the throat and upper body. He died shortly after medics' arrived.

    The case has grim similarities to that of British student Meredith Kercher who was killed in Italy a few days before Mr Mugnier.

    Davies said that before the killing she had had several pints of beer and shots of spirits at Sullivans, an Irish pub in St Germain-en-Laye, the prosperous Paris suburb where she was brought up, and felt 'I was going to do something stupid'.

    She had also swallowed four different types of anti-depressant pills.

    The court heard that the combination of alcohol and drugs would have made her aggressive.

    Later, however, Davies said all memories of the knife attack ' disappeared into a black hole'.

    Versailles Criminal Court, near Paris, heard how the knife used to kill Mr Mugnier had been used by Davies in a suicide attempt three months earlier.

    If found guilty of 'voluntary homicide without premeditation' she faces up to 30 years in prison.

    She does not have to enter a plea under French law.

    However, she told the court: 'If I had the intention of killing someone I would not have called the emergency services afterwards and I would do my best to show I did not do it. So I don't think this was voluntary.'

    She said she had met Mugnier - a local jobless man nicknamed 'Funtime' - on the night of the attack, picking him up in the pub for sex.

    At the time she was depressed following a string of personal setbacks, including splitting up with her boyfriend of four years, her parents' divorce, and a failed modelling career, she said.

    'I didn't know how to bond with other people. I mechanically changed friends every year,' Davies added, admitting she often took cocaine.

    Uncle: Defence Minister Quentin Davies

    Monique Henry, her mother, described her as 'an adolescent who was disturbed by family difficulties' and who grew into a woman 'unable to face day-to-day life'.

    Mrs Henry said that as a teenager her daughter 'did not know when to stop drinking'.

    The literature professor acknowledged that her separation from Davies' father Richard - a British banker who left his family to move to Italy with his mistress - deeply upset her daughter.

    She added that in the months before the crime, Davies had taken to cutting her own arms and legs. Her father was also in court and admitted he had been an absent

    father. Psychiatrists told the court that Davies' personality difficulties meant her responsibility for the attack was diminished.

    Detectives believe the crime may have been inspired by the Meredith Kercher murder.

    An alcohol and drug fuelled sex game led to the the death of Meredith in the city of Perugia, with American Amanda Knox, Italian Raffaele Sollecito, and Ivory Coast drifter Rudy Guede using a knife to kill her.

    A police spokesman said at the time of Davies' arrest: 'She spoke to us of the case in Italy and it may be that she was inspired by what happened there.'

    The trial is due to end today. In 2007 Davies' uncle, Quentin Davies, defected from the Conservatives to Labour.

    The MP for Grantham and Stamford has since been made a junior defence minister.
