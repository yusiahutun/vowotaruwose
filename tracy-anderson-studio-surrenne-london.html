    London’s latest longevity-focused members’ club Surrenne opens this Sunday, the 14th of April, dedicated to enabling science-backed wellness rituals.

    Below new hotel The Emory, the chic subterranean space sprawls four floors. There's The Wet Zone, with a striking 22-metre pool, hot and cold exposure treatments and health café boasting offerings curated by nutritionist, Rose Ferguson; The Treatments and Longevity Floor comprising a medically-led longevity lounge for practitioner appointments, an active recovery zone dedicated to biohacking (from microbiome mapping to epigenetic testing) and seven state-of-the-art treatment rooms for high-performance facials and massages; The Fitness Floor featuring personal training and custom diagnostics, an advanced gym and recovery station plus an interactive digital studio streaming on-demand classes; and The Tracy Anderson Floor, an innovative studio and retail space devoted to the fitness entrepreneur’s expertise. Phew.

    “Bringing the Tracy Anderson Studio was a perfect opportunity due to the synergy between our method and the UK's health-conscious culture,” the famed fitness pioneer tells us. “Surrenne's philosophy resonates with ours as they prioritise evidence-based health solutions.” Her signature classes (think strategic muscle exhaustion sequences, enhanced by an isokinetic band system) are amplified by the studio’s bouncy ‘Super G Floor’ – which minimises impact while promising triple the calorie burn over an hour.

    Surrenne

    Anderson's method may be ever evolving – leaning into the latest advancements in science and invention – but “for 25 years, we've stayed true to our mission, avoiding passing trends,” she tells us. It’s something her unwaveringly loyal clientele speaks to. Indeed, one of her most famous – Gwyneth Paltrow – still practices it daily. “I've been a Tracy Anderson devotee for 17 years and I really love her method,” the actor recently told Bazaar of her morning workout routine.

    Related Story

    What does she credit such commitment to? “My method's longevity is attributed to the transformative results it yields, both physically and mentally,” Anderson offers. “Beyond weight loss or sculpted abs, it fosters self-acceptance and liberation, enabling individuals to embrace themselves fully.” The choreography that she designs encompasses the entire human experience, the entrepreneur explains – “I aim to create balance where there's imbalance, integrating the power of the mind with the body.” She believes that fostering this connection is key to promoting long-term harmonious health as much as achieving an A-list-esqe physique.

    “Living long is about being resilient and my method nurtures that ability into a superpower”

    Tracy Anderson

    “Living long is about being able to be resilient and my method definitely nurtures that ability into a superpower,” she shares. Though, naturally, “intelligent movement” is only part of the puzzle. “Cultivate meaningful relationships, practice self-love and nourish yourself with whole, real foods to support overall wellbeing,” are more longevity mottos the 49-year-old lives by.

    Clearly, the Tracy Anderson Method is a perfect fit for Surrenne, which takes a meticulously measured approach to luxury wellness. Those who can, definitely should.

    An annual membership at Knightsbridge members’ club Surrenne costs £10,000, plus a joining fee of £5,000. For further details on applying for membership to Surrenne contact: membership@surrenne.com or visit @surrenne on Instagram.
