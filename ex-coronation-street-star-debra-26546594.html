    Corrie's Debra Stephenson 'once stole Katie Price's boobs'

    Former Coronation Street actress Debra Stephenson has confessed to &quot;stealing Katie Price's boobs&quot;. The star was featured on Steph's Packed Lunch on Thursday (March 24) where she made the shocking revelation.

    18 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Former Coronation Street actress Debra Stephenson has confessed to "stealing Katie Price's boobs".

    The star was featured on Steph's Packed Lunch on Thursday (March 24) where she made the shocking revelation.

    She told host Steph McGovern and TV guest John Whaite: "I took Katie Price's boobs...

    "It was a costume and they were the latex boobs that went into the costume and I thought they would be handy as a fancy dress and I haven't got rid of them because I didn't know how to throw them away. I mean, how do I recycle latex boobs?"

    Strictly star John then chimed in to say: "You should recycle them by giving them to a young aspiring drag queen - they're expensive, breastplates."

    Then doing her best Katie Price impression, Debra jokingly replied: "I've rceyled these few times anyways."

    This comes a day after Louise Minchin opened up about her exit from BBC Breakfast on Steph's show.

    Louise joined her former BBC co-host and friend Steph McGovern, 39, on Wednesday's (March 23) episode of Steph's show.

    The pair had worked together on the show for over eight years together.

    Louise then opened up about her reasons for quitting last September and delcared it was "a long time coming."

    She said: "My boss at the time was very surprised when I told him I was going to leave. Lots of people who watch BBC Breakfast were really surprised too."

    Steph recalled her reaction to Louise's exit and added: "I was shocked. Normally I know what is going on before everything happens."

    Comic Russell Kane then chipped in and joked that she should have announced she was leaving the show live on air before telling anyone else.

    Louise scoffed and said: "Do you know what, I was really tempted.

    "But I thought, let's manage this in a way that is nice. But they were really nice to me when I left and I've left on good terms."
