    Who is Nicole from BMF in real life?

    Nicole Flenory, famous as Big Meech's sister, is the youngest member of the Flenory family. She was born to Charles and Lucille Flenory on October 18, 1974, in Dartmouth Street, Detroit, Michigan.

    Nicole's father, Charles Flenory, was a guitarist for Keith Dominion and House of God. In 1997, he wrote the song Jump for Joy for the Campell Brothers. Nicole's mother, Lucille, is the CEO of Building More Families (BMF) in Michigan, United States.

    Rise and fall of the Flenory brothers

    Nicole Flenory's parents couldn't provide the best for their family – they depended on welfare and food stamps. The hard life pushed the Flenory brothers into the narcotics game. They started selling $50 bags of narcotics on the Detroit streets during their high school years in the late 80s and were known as “50 Boyz.”

    By 2000, Big Meech and Southwest T were running a large organisation distributing multi-kilogram narcotics in several US states, including Tennessee, North Carolina, Alabama, Ohio, Florida, California, Missouri, Louisiana, Mississippi, Georgia, Michigan, and Kentucky.

    In the early 2000s, Big Meech founded BMF Entertainment as a record label and promotion agency for hip-hop music. However, the primary role of BMF Entertainment was to clean money generated from narcotics distribution. Around this time, the Flenory organisation formally named itself the Black Mafia Family.

    They associated with several high-profile hip-hop artists including Fabolous, Young Jeezy, P Diddy, Jay Z, TI and Trina.

    By around 2003, the Flenory brothers had fallen out and rarely spoke to each other. Demetrius Flenory took over the main distribution centre in Atlanta, while Terry Felony relocated to Los Angeles to head his organisation.

    Throughout the years, the DEA monitored the brothers’ phone calls, and by the time charges were being filed against them, the government had 900 typed pages of their wiretapped conversations.

    What happened to Nicole Flenory?

    Big Meech's sister, Nicole Flenory, never had any direct links or dealings with the Black Mafia Family, so she was never investigated or charged. She lives a private life with her husband in Lincoln Park, Michigan.

    There isn’t much information either about Nicole Flenory's husband, Steele. However, Nicole and Steele share two children: son Demetrius Steele, an entrepreneur and chef, and son Dilan, a painting artist.

    In addition, Nicole is active on Instagram, where she often shares pictures of her children and nephew, Michael "Lil Meech." In addition, she has made several red-carpet appearances because of the TV series Black Mafia Family.
