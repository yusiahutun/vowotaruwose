    03 Oct 2024, 00:00

    Blame it on Mercury retrograde or just Megan Fox being Megan Fox, but the actress just debuted a new hair color on Instagram, checking yet another shade of the rainbow off her list. In the post, Fox shared a trio of photos that showed off a new pale-blue hair color and sleek bob. Tagging her team, including hair stylist Dimitris Giannetos and makeup artist Jenna Kristina, Fox had a soft, bronze smoky eye and super-glossy overlined lips to give her hair a moment to shine.

    The blue makeover included lighter pieces to frame her face and dark brown roots. While Fox is no stranger to going bold with her hair color, the shift was an unexpected flex as we head into the summer, where celebrities usually take the warm weather as an opportunity to go blonde.

    "Entering my jedi era," she captioned the carousel.

    Before stepping into her blue era, Fox's hair was pink. At the 2024 People's Choice Awards, she presented an award with Joe Manganiello and wore it long and wavy — and repeated the look for an event in Las Vegas.

    And before that, Fox had a cherry-red bob with a set of heavy bangs. She showed off that style while out and about in New York as she promoted her book of poems, Pretty Boys Are Poisonous: Poems: A Collection of Fucked Up Fairy Tales. In the book, which is now a New York Times Bestseller, she opened up about having a miscarriage.

    “There is an ultrasound by your side of the bed, 10 weeks and 1 day … do you think that if she could have, she would have left a suicide note?” Fox wrote, in one poem, per Page Six. “But now I have to say goodbye. I close my eyes and imagine holding you tight against my chest as they rip you from my insides."

    She spoke about the experience during an appearance on Good Morning America, saying that the miscarriage sent her and her Machine Gun Kelly on a "very wild journey" both together and separately. “I had never been through anything like that before in my life. I have three kids, so it was very difficult for both of us," Fox said. "And it sent us on a very wild journey together and separately, and together and apart, and together and apart, and together and apart, trying to navigate, 'What does this mean? Why did this happen?'"
