    Naruto is one of the best-selling manga series in history. The manga series tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja looking for recognition from his peers and wanting to become his village leader. The narrative has been adapted numerous times in magazines and television productions, making it one of the most prevalent in anime. Despite the series being based on Japanese culture and characters, you have probably noticed that there are black people. Hard to believe, right? So, who are the black Naruto characters?

    Are there any black Naruto characters? It is hard to spot dark-skinned characters in anime, but things have changed. Anime is primarily made in Japan, but it is celebrated worldwide with growing audiences from diverse cultural and racial backgrounds. And some of your favourite anime and movies acknowledge this, hence the inclusion of black characters.

    Naruto, one of the most renowned in the niche, has set a great example by having black characters. So, are there black female Naruto characters?

    Popular black Naruto characters

    Reviewers have always had good words for Naruto’s plot and character development. The manga has an amazing storyline and well-executed fight scenes. That is why it is one of the best-selling manga series, having sold over 250 million volumes.

    Another amazing thing about the manga series is that it has an amazing collection of black characters. So, who is the black dude in Naruto?

    1. Kidomaru

    Kidomaru is one of the most outspoken black male anime characters in Naruto. He is a member of Sound Village (Otogakure) and Sound Flare. The character's complexion, of course, is dark-skinned, which makes him a black character. Kidomaru also has a ponytail of shaggy black hair and black eyes.

    The character regarded battles as a game and always fought with a laid-back demeanour. Therefore, Kidomaru loved combat and found powerful opponents entertaining. He lost the battle with the Konoha genius Neji.

    2. Killer B

    Is Killer Bee from Naruto black? The character is black, alright. Killer B's rhymes were whack; however, his skills as the jinchū-riki of the Eight-Tails were amazing. The Naruto character's story is interesting, as it is one of overcoming adversity during his early years.

    Killer B was shunned by his villagers and rapped as a coping mechanism to keep it together through it all. One of the most remarkable things about Killer B is that he is powerful and proud of who he is despite the challenges he went through.

    The Naruto character has a muscular build, a goatee, oval-shaped sunglasses, and white-coloured forehead protection. Killer B's origin story of overcoming prejudice is a great example for people who might face the same in society. And he shows just how to deal with it. With a smile and a song.

    3. Darui

    Darui is the strongest ninja in the Hidden Cloud. He is tall and dark-skinned with a bulbous nose. Darui also possesses bored-looking eyes and shaggy white hair covering his left eye. He has that ninja look, always dressed in a sleeveless uniform with loose-fitting slacks.

    The character is overly apologetic, leading him to say sorry often. And that is why he comes out as understanding. Despite these soft qualities, Darui is still a strong and skilful shinobi.

    4. Omoi

    Omoi is a cautious and thoughtful character in Naruto, who is dark-skinned. He has short, spiky, white hair and dark eyes with eyelashes arching upwards from the corners.

    Compared with his teammate, Karui, Omoi can be considered more level-headed as he is more careful. He puts much thought before making a move. However, Darui has a vivid imagination and can exaggerate minor occurrences' outcomes.

    If you have wondered, "Who is the black girl in Naruto?" here's your answer. It is the gorgeous Chocho Akimichi. She is dark-skinned with amber-coloured eyes and a larger build. She inherited her brown hair from her father and wore it in two spiky ponytails with a pink hair band.
