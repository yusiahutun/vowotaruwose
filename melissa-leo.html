    Melissa Leo’s Bio, Wiki

    Melissa Leo was born as Melissa Chessington Leo in 1960 in Manhattan, New York, the United States. She celebrates her birthday on every 14th of September. Born in the U.S., she holds an American nationality and belongs to the white ethnicity.

    Her father, Arnold Leo III is famous as an editor at Grove Press, fisherman and used to work for the East Hampton Baymen’s Association as a spokesperson. Whereas, her mother, Margaret Chessington is an American teacher.

    Similarly, Leo’s parents got divorced when she was in her twenties. She grew up along with her brother, Erik Leo. Also, she has an aunt named Christine Leo Roussel, an American author, photographer, and historian.

    Since an early age, Melissa was so much interested in participating in acting scenes and plays as well. She also performed with the Beard and Puppet Theatre Company. Following, she attended Bellows Falls High School in Vermont and later joined acting at Mountview Academy of Theatre Arts in London. However, she didn’t graduate from college.

    Melissa Leo’s Career (Movies)

    Following the dream of getting established in the film industry, Melissa Leo made her first acting debut in 1984 in the American soap opera, All My Children. During the 2000s, she appeared in the film, Hide and Seek alongside Dakota Fanning, Famke Janssen, and Robert De Niro.

    Similarly, Leo won the Bronze Wrangler for her performance in French-American film, The Three of Melquiades Estrada. After playing in over 20 films, she shocked the mass with her role of Ray Eddy in the American crime-drama film, Frozen River.

    Her other film credits are Welcome to the Rileys, The Dry Land, The Space Between, Conviction, Red State, Whey Stop Now, Prisoners, The Equalizer, Novitiate, The Ashram and so on. Also, she made her appearances in TV series such as Miami Vice, Nasty Boys, Law & Order, Shark, Louie, Broad City, Heartstrings and many others.
