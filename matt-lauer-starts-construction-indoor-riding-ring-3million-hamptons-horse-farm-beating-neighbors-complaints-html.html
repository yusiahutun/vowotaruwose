    Matt Lauer builds indoor riding ring on his $3million Hamptons horse farm after beating back neighbors' complaints

    By Laura Pullman for the Daily Mail

    Published: 09:58 EDT, 18 October 2012 | Updated: 15:43 EDT, 18 October 2012

    • e-mail

    View
    comments


    Horsing around: Construction has been halted on Today Show host Matt Lauer's horse farm in Long Island

    Matt Lauer's renovation of a multi-million dollar property in the Hamptons is officially underway as he prepares to make the property a horse farm.

    His decision to turn the $3.5million property into a farm for 30 horses has angered residents living around the area and many attempted to prevent the construction.

    Their complaints were overturned, however, as Lauer has officially begun to build an indoor training ring for the animals.

    The New York Post reports that in addition to the training ring, renovations are underway on the existing barn along with the new barn that he plans to use.

    Because of the nature of the land, which is located on a wildlife preserve, the Today Show host is not legally allowed to build a home on the 30-acre plot.

    Instead he is dedicating a portion of the property as a small nursery where grown trees are being replanted.

    The battle over the fate of the land, located on Deerfield Road in the exclusive town of Water Mill, has been ongoing and neighbours are unlikely to stop their mumblings.

    Local officials are getting complaints from residents who say the farm will be too intrusive and could cause traffic problems.
    Lauer, who just recently signed a $25million contract with NBC, purchased the property in April.

    Situated in the area of Water Mill, the property was previously a tree nursery and is just down the road from the news anchor’s $15million mansion.

    Pony paradise: Lauer plans to transform the 40-acres of farmland into an equestrian's dream with an indoor arena, paddocks and barns

    Luxury pad: The new farm is minutes from Lauer's $15million mansion, seen here surrounded by woodland

    The property listing describes the land as 'magnificent' and details the 'incredible' views of the ocean and surrounding greenery.

    When completed, the luxury stables will reportedly boast an indoor arena, paddocks and barns.

    But neighbours told the New York Post that the property will cause too many issues into the area because of its size.

    Resident Dee Hunter told the newspaper: 'It’s just too big.

    'They presented this as a little old family farm. But there is supposed to be room for 30 horses? Do they each ride six horses?'

    Investing: Lauer is gradually adding to his properties in the famously upmarket Hamptons

    Another neighbour, Harriet Whittenberg, told the Post that the plan will impede on residents' lifestyles.

    'Just because Lauer and his wife have this dream doesn’t mean that you can take down the quality of life for everyone,' she said.

    The price of the property itself is just spare change for Lauer - the highest paid TV newsman - but many more millions will be spent transforming the sprawling 40-acre farmland into a pony paradise if the plan is approved.

    Lauer’s wife, Annette Roque, a Dutch model, and their three children will be pleased with the project as they are believed to be the real equestrians of the family.

    The area is a favourite of celebrities with Richard Gere, Christie Brinkley and Kelly Ripa all a stone's throw away from the farm.
