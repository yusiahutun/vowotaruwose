    Pros and cons of crochet braids on your natural hair

    Natural hair is all the rage in town, but if you are looking for a way to take a break from the combs, consider crocheting. Learning the pros and cons of crochet braids is a great place to start if you want to rock the look.

    Natural hair is all the rage in town, but if you are looking for a way to take a break from the combs, consider crocheting. Learning the pros and cons of crochet braids is a great place to start if you want to rock the look.

    Crotchet braids are slowly regaining their popularity. They not only offer you convenience, but also allow you to pull off a Rihanna or Beyoncé-like hairstyle effortlessly.

    They are a definite must-try in every woman’s salon journal. They are incredible, lightweight and most of all, perfect all-year-round. Even with all the fantastic elements that come with crochet braids, one question lingers, are crochet braids damaging to hair?

    Well, here are some key crochet braids pros and cons that you should consider.

    Advantages of crochet braids

    So, what are some of the merits that come with crotchet braids?

    They are cheap

    Unlike braids and weaves, crochet braids are affordable, especially since you can always install them yourself. By just learning the steps on YouTube, you can fix them yourself and avoid the extra costs of going to the salon.

    They are versatile

    Crochet braids offer you a myriad of possibilities. You can have the Rita Ora wavy hairstyle, the Rihanna bob, and the popular afro punk style. You can also opt to have them textured or fine – it all depends on your taste and preferences.

    They also help you conceal your natural hair, thus giving you the freedom to play with the colour range. So if you are a Lady Gaga kind of girl, crochet braids will be your favourite style season in, season out.

    They are comfortable and quick to install

    Installing crochet braids is effortless and straightforward if you are a master at the technique. You have to attach hair extensions to your cornrows hair using a latch hook. You do not need any glue or a needle.

    If you want to practice some DIY styles, there are many tutorials online to help you work it out, step by step.

    They favour length retention

    Crochet braids hair growth results are impressive too. Since you braid your natural hair underneath, you do not experience the constant pulling and stretching. Your natural hair gets the time to grow.

    Disadvantages of crochet braids

    In as much as there is a good side of having crotchet braids, there is also the downside part of it.

    They can stunt your hair growth

    The most vital warning about crochet braids is that they can stunt your hair growth. With the fast-paced economy, very few people forget to wash their hair. When you do not take care of your crochet braids, your scalp suffers, leading to dry hair and, eventually, stunted growth.

    Crochet braids can cause tension on your scalp

    When you stick the latch through your scalp, you are parting your hair. Adding the braided twist or curls through a tiny part, under the braided cornrow, you pull some off the delicate strands, thus adding some weight to your hair. It gets worse if you want to have a full set of hair with your favourite crochet braid style.

    Adding over ten pieces to hair makes it bulky, overwhelms your scalp, and eventually causes too much tension. You might not notice it at first, but with time, you will have spaces in between your hair.

    Getting the perfect curl is challenging

    Some crochet braids brand ambassadors go over the top when marketing these braids. Most of them give you the basics of what is involved in getting the perfect crochet braid curl, but they do not go into details on the tie and work it takes to achieve the ideal curl. You have to have the braids rodded severally, dip them in water, and set them for 24 hours before installing them.

    Since stylists do not allow clients to take the rods home, this process might be challenging, resulting in frizzy curls instead of the advertised "perfect curl."

    Hair snapping issues

    Is your hairline struggling? Have you been investing in wigs to hide the receding forehead caused by hair shedding?

    Well, when you install already pre-twisted crochet braids, you are likely to experience hair snapping when installing the hair – a process that eventually leads to hair shedding. It is one of the reasons most women today have a receding hairline.

    Frequently asked questions

    Find out answers to some of the most answered questions about crochet braids.

    Are crochet braids damaging to your hair?

    Yes, some are. You can avoid this by ensuring proper care, maintenance, and treating your hair with respect before deciding to braid it down. Invest in rosemary oil, jojoba, coconut oil, or Shea butter. They might cost you some bucks, but they will leave your hair healthy, making it easy to crochet it from time to time.

    It would be best if you also stopped leaving your crochet braids for over eight weeks. It renders your hair weak, thus causing breakage and damage to your hairline.

    Are crochet braids a good protective style?

    Yes, they are. As long as you show your hair some TLC, you can have fantastic results with crochet braids. Therefore, before crocheting, ensure that you regularly shampoo, condition, and treat it. You can also make some home hair treatments like mixing an egg with avocado. It is a disgusting mixture, but it will make your hair healthy, and you will become the hair goddess that you have always wanted to be.

    Can you go swimming with crochet braids?

    Yes, you can enjoy your vacation on the coastal beaches or the Maldives with crochet braids. If you have a tight curl pattern, you make sure you do a full crochet braid installation instead of leaving any hair out.

    How long can you keep crochet braids?

    Crochet braids last from four to eight weeks. Leaving them for more than eight weeks causes breakage and sometimes shedding. When it comes to how long to wait between crochet braids, most stylists recommend waiting for at least a week before installations so that your scalp can breathe.
