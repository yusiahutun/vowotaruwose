    YouTuber BeardMeatsFood takes on 50 burger challenge but staff make him do one thing before he goe

    27 Jul 2024, 00:00

    He's demolished the world's biggest creme egg, made light work of a 20-inch baguette and even seen off the legendary Octuple Bypass Burger at the Heart Attack Grill in Las Vegas.

    For his latest challenge, YouTuber Adam Moran, headed to Norway in search of some traditional food, but found himself at the American-style Groovy Diner in Tonsberg. Not for the faint-hearted, participants get an hour to complete the Ultimate Groovy Burger Challenge - a gigantic burger made up of 10 patties served with fries and washed down with a huge milkshake.

    Those who succeed get the 699kr (£50) meal for free and a place on the Wall of Champions. The only rule is, you must remain seated for 15 minutes after the epic calorie-laden feast.

    The 38-year-old from Leeds, better known as BeardMeatsFood, has earned the title of Britain’s number one competitive eater. His YouTube channel has 3.65m followers and he’s so popular that he’s taken out a £1 million insurance policy on his stomach and taste buds.

    As a qualified personal trainer, he reckons that competitive eating together with two hours’ daily weight training has improved his body-fat ratio.

    After setting up his cameras, staff served up the massive meat tower propped up by a basket of fries and served with a milkshake almost as tall.

    Hearing that the quickest time was 20 minutes, Adam states: "He was a NorwegIan guy right? If I break his record, I'm going to feel bad."

    Just before a waitress counts down to his challenge, he states: "Probably be a bit jittery but let's get underway."

    Eating the meal two burgers at a time, Adam soon polishes it off.

    "I'm eating this thing with almost surgical precision. Not a scrap of debris in sight," he states.

    Just three minutes in and a member of staff can be heard saying: "I think the record is in jeopardy."

    And as Adam nears the end of the burger tower, he asks: "You sure you get one hour to do this?"

    The waitress replies: "This is the difference between Norwegian people and people from other countries."

    Adam laughs replying: "We're not all like this, believe me."

    From the kitchen, a member of staff sees the emptying plate and jokes: "keep them coming."

    Adam says on his video: "Admittedly, this isn't the toughest challenge I've ever done but it's all about the adventure if you ask me."

    With the burger gone in less than nine minutes, he downs the milkshake in two gulps, finishing the meal in an impressive 12 minutes and 48 seconds.

    Staff then tell him he has to remain in his seat for 15 minutes to make sure he's not sick.

    "That's a cool rule, I like it. While I'm here for 15 minutes, do you have a dessert menu?," Adam replies, much to their disbelief as he orders a chocolate cake with ice cream.

    As he leaves the diner, Adam states: "I feel bad for the Norwegian guy but the food challenges in Europe are quite easy, it's got to be said.

    "I might even have enough room for a bit of Norwegian scran back at the hotel."

    The video has already amassed more than 1.5m views and 84k likes.

    Fans were quick to share their thoughts with one commenting: "Was that a challenge or a quick snack?"

    One said: "God only knows where you put the food, Adam, although it's great to watch and marvel."

    The previous winner @Settpasmaken even commented: "Wow! It's an honour to be included in your video Adam. Not really surprised that you smashed my time."
