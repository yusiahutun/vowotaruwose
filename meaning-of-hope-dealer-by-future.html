    Meaning of Hope Dealer by Future

    17 Apr 2024, 00:00

    In Future's captivating and introspective song, 'Hope Dealer', the Atlanta rapper delves into the complexities of life, identity, and the pursuit of hope. The song, produced by Echelon Knoxx, offers a unique perspective on the concept of hope and its correlation with selling. Through thought-provoking lyrics, Future crafts a narrative that explores the transformative power of hope and the contrasting narratives of selling dope and selling hope.One standout lyric in the song is, "Before I sell my soul, I would sell a boat to Peter Storm I stay afloat." Amidst a world that often pushes individuals towards compromising their values, Future chooses to prioritize maintaining his integrity. This lyric poetically conveys the message that before succumbing to the allure of selling out, one should relentlessly cling onto their values and keep pushing forward, even if it means venturing into uncharted territories.Continuing along this theme, Future astutely observes, "God is what I believe, that's who I came for. I ain't in here for the bread, but Lord knows we poor." Here, he acknowledges the significance of faith and its role in seeking hope and salvation. It suggests that a higher power can provide the answers and strength needed to endure difficult times. Future recognizes that material wealth alone is not the ultimate source of contentment; rather, it is spiritual fulfillment that truly brings a sense of peace and hope.Building upon the contrast between selling dope and selling hope, Future presents the line, "I'm never gonna sell my soul, but I can sell you hope." This lyric ingeniously expresses the idea that hope can be just as powerful, if not more, than any physical product. It underscores the notion that hope has the potential to change lives, uplift souls, and inspire individuals to overcome their circumstances. In a world often shadowed by despair, the ability to offer hope is a priceless commodity, one that transcends the superficiality of material possessions.Furthermore, Future exposes the harsh realities of life in the following line, "Sometimes it's the haters that wish you well. I said they can take their wishes to a wishing well." Here, he unravels the perplexing dichotomy of human nature, where individuals can both harbor ill intentions towards one's success yet hypocritically offer well wishes. It highlights the importance of discernment, urging individuals to be cautious of those who claim to wish them well but secretly harbor envy and negativity. The lyric acts as a cautionary reminder to navigate relationships with discernment, ensuring that one does not fall prey to false hopes or manipulative intentions.In the outro, Future remarks, "And the truth is, everybody seems dope, looking for a fix but they don't know. They can't figure it out. They don't know what it is, but they want hope." This lyric poignantly captures the universal quest for hope that permeates through human existence. All humans, regardless of their circumstances or background, yearn for something intangible that can provide solace and purpose. Future astutely observes that hope remains an enigma for most, an elusive concept that is difficult to define, yet an undeniable longing that resides in the hearts of all.Overall, 'Hope Dealer' delves into the profound themes of integrity, spirituality, discernment, and the universal desire for hope. Through insightful lyrics, Future challenges listeners to reflect on their own journey for purpose, while highlighting the transformative power that hope possesses. By eschewing the conventional narrative of selling dope for material gain, Future passionately advocates for the salesmanship of hope, emphasizing that it is in the pursuit of true, genuine hope where one finds lasting fulfillment and meaning. So, let's heed Future's call, push play, and embark on a journey towards a future bright with hope.
