    Julietta Compagno Skoog

    Julietta Skoog is well known as the eldest of the Compagno siblings. She was born in the mid-1970s in Oakland, Califonia, USA. Julietta's parents are John Compagno and Katherine Bertsch.

    Julietta is of mixed ethnicity. Her mother, Katherine Bertsch, is of English, Bohemian, and Baden-Wurttemberg descent, while her father, John Compagno, is of Sicilian descent. Julietta grew up in El Cerrito, Contra Costa County, California, United States.

    Education background

    Julietta attained her elementary education in California before moving to Seattle to join the University of Washington from 1994 to 1998. She then joined Seattle University's School of Psychology from 2002 to 2005. Julietta graduated from Seattle University with an Ed.S Degree in School Psychology and a Master's in School Counseling.

    Career

    According to her LinkedIn profile, Julietta Skoog has worked as a parent coach, school psychologist and positive discipline trainer with Seattle Public Schools since 2005. Some of her expertise includes autism, early child development, learning disabilities, behaviour disorders and anxiety.

    Julietta also leads classroom lessons, meetings and friendship groups to encourage discipline and social thinking. In addition, she is the co-founder of Sproutable, a science-backed online parenting insight for pregnancy to prekindergarten that helps to multitask sleep-deprived parents everywhere.

    Personal life

    Julietta is married and has two children. Her husband, Jon Skoog, has been her companion since 2008, when they married. Their children are Violet Avery Skoog and Josphine Harper.
