    Paris Hilton just made grandma-core look ultra chic.

    Last night, she stepped out in New York City on Monday for “Paris Hilton’s 11:11 Media and Catapulting Brands Into the Cultural Zeitgeist,” a fireside chat event that was part of Advertising Week. The heiress—who is CEO and co-founder of 11:11 Media—stood out from the crowd in an elegant ensemble that put a subtle twist on a major vintage trend.

    Hilton wore a lacy black minidress with a nude underlay, ornate gold buttons down the center, boxy black pockets, and scalloped short sleeves. The dress also featured a white Peter Pan collar with lace trim, a nod to a trend that dominated the 1930s before eventually being resuscitated for modern fashion lovers in the 2010s.

    She finished off the look with black opaque tights, studded black pumps, a gold bracelet, and stud earrings.

    Shutterstock

    Hilton recently had a fashion-filled month, having jetted across the world to sit front row at multiple designer shows during Fashion Week.

    She made a colorful appearance at Chanel’s Spring/Summer 2024 runway show, where she channeled Elle Woods in a black skirt suit trimmed with fuzzy hot-pink fur on the hemline, waist, and torso. The set included a zip-up vest and a glossy satin skirt with a bedazzled Chanel logo belt. She wore the piece with a patterned sheer mesh long-sleeved top, black pumps with a rosette adornment on the toe, a shiny quilted purse, and tons of Chanel jewelry, including layers of gold-chain-and-pearl-strand necklaces and sparkling 5 pendant drop earrings.
