In just a few clicks, you can download and watch some of the most legendary movies ever produced.

    In this article, we'll talk about what the phrase "public domain" actually means. Free public domain movies online? It sounds almost too good to be true.

    What Is Public Domain?

    In the world of intellectual property, content in the public domain has no owner aside from the governing body enforcing the system. In America, intellectual property becomes a part of the public domain 95 years after being created, if nobody renews the rights to the property.

    A non-copyright movie may precede copyright law as it exists today; films like this also fall into the public domain because they were never truly copyrighted in the first place. Many silent or black and white public domain films are available online for this reason exactly. Some no-copyright movies are in the public domain because the creator declined to copyright their work at all.

    Aside from free copyright movies, you can find TV shows, music, and even free stock images to enjoy in the public domain. Software created before 1974 slides into this category, but you'll still want to be careful when downloading. Occasionally, copyright status changes, so always double-check before streaming a public domain download.

Granted, many of these royalty-free movies will feel charmingly dated, but that's half the fun of watching movies copyright-free.

    1. Public Domain Torrents

    As the name suggests, Public Domain Torrents offers torrents of public domain movies and shows. This treasure trove of copyright-free movies and shows includes tons of cult classics.

    Although many reputable sites allow you to download public domain movies, Public Domain Torrents remains one of the top sources. On the left-hand sidebar, there's a fantastic public domain movie database. It's organized by category; you'll find genres including action/adventure, animation, sci-fi/fantasy, and westerns. Everything on their no-copyright movies list is available in AVI, iPod MP4, and PlayStation Portable MP4.

    2. Archive.org

    Over on Archive.org, you'll find a thriving catalog of public domain downloads. You can sort its public domain movies list by genre, year, and more. Unlike Public Domain Torrents, copyright-free movies are available as either a direct download or as a torrent. The format options available to you will depend on the title in question.

    While Archive.org boasts a tremendous catalog, you will find it ridden with multiple copies of the same movies. In the best-case scenario, this means that you might find a free copyright movie at a higher resolution than you were expecting if you're willing to slough through the duplicates.

    Still, it's an incredible source for TV shows, public domain music, and movies, copyright-free and ready to rumble. Whereas Public Domain Torrents only hosts downloads, you can stream straight from the Archive.org website.

    3. Public Domain Movies

    Public Domain Movies is another very impressive public domain movie database, packed with quality films like 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea. You can stream royalty-free movies without downloading them, but there are download options available, as well.

    Public Domain Movies provides a substantive synopsis and cast list for most of the copyright-free Hollywood movies that it offers. Though the user interface is aesthetically pleasing, one unfortunate drawback is that there is no search feature.

    Nevertheless, sorting through this list of free copyright movies by genre will help you navigate their extensive collection of copyright-free movies.     4. Public Domain Flix

    Though Public Domain Flix appears to deal mainly in embedded YouTube videos, it's still a nifty resource if you've got a copyright-free movie in mind that you would like to indulge in. You can stream thousands of public domain movies, all aggregated here for your convenience. Site navigation is simple thanks to a robust search feature. There's also a brief synopsis of each no-copyright movie included.

    The only thing that Public Domain Flix is missing is some sort of download function. Despite this, its excellent layout and plot descriptions still make it a phenomenal public domain movies site.

    5. Classic Cinema Online

    Classic Cinema Online hosts many classics, including plenty of public domain cartoon movies available for your perusal. You'll also find public domain television shows, syndicated for you digitally from here until the end of time.

    This is another glorified list of copyright-free movies hosted on YouTube, but we can't complain. Aggregators like these make coordinating movie night a cinch. We watched Gulliver's Travels in HD without paying a single cent. It was awesome.
