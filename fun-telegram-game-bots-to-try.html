    8 Fun Telegram Game Bots You Should Try

    Telegram is not just your regular messaging app; it's a trove of exciting features that keep users engaged and entertained. One of the coolest features that sets Telegram apart is its collection of game bots.

    27 Apr 2024, 00:00

    Telegram is not just your regular messaging app; it's a trove of exciting features that keep users engaged and entertained. One of the coolest features that sets Telegram apart is its collection of game bots.

    These Telegram game bots offer a wide range of interactive and challenging games you can play alone or with your friends in group chats. Let's look at some of the most popular Telegram game bots worth trying.

    What Are Telegram Game Bots?

    Telegram game bots enhance your chatting experience by adding fun and interactive games right into your group chats or direct messages. These virtual entertainers offer a range of games that can challenge your skills and entertain you and your friends. The best part is that they're easy to use, and you can start playing with just a few taps or clicks.

    Some bots require you to add them as members to your group chat, while others are inline bots that you can use without adding them. You can summon these inline bots with a simple command and enjoy their entertainment within your private or group chats.

    1. Would You Rather

    The Would You Rather bot lets you play the classic Would You Rather game with your friends on Telegram. Whether you're looking for thought-provoking questions or cheeky NSFW ones, Would You Rather Bot has got you covered. Just type @RatherGameBot in a private or group chat to kick off the game. Select the question type—normal or NSFW—and brace yourself for tricky decisions.

    The bot will present you with two options to choose from. Each participant picks their preferred choice, and once everyone has voted or the timer has expired, the bot reveals the results, showing who would rather what.

    Would You Rather bot is a fantastic way to spark conversations and share a good laugh with your pals. As the bot works inline, there's no need to add it to a group chat.

    2. Tic Tac Toe

    This bot brings the timeless Tic Tac Toe game to Telegram. You can use it to play alone or with friends. Tic Tac Toe is an inline bot, meaning you don't have to add it to your group chat to play. Just call @xoBot in your chat, pick your side, and the game begins.

    The bot is quite basic, considering the straightforward nature of the game. The game is simple enough to make your own version of Tic Tac Toe with some coding knowledge.

    3. Werewolf

    Werewolf is a thrilling game of deception and deduction similar to Mafia. Each player is randomly assigned the role of either a villager or a werewolf. The werewolves secretly select one villager to eliminate during the night. The villagers must work together to find and eradicate the werewolves before it's too late.

    The Werewolf bot is perfect for groups with five or more players. You can add the werewolf bot to your group chat and start a game there. Players will see their roles and interact with the game through their private chat with the bot.

    Werewolf bot supports multiple languages, ensuring everyone can participate and enjoy the game to the fullest.

    4. Chat Against Humanity

    Inspired by the popular card game Cards Against Humanity, this bot brings the same laughter and absurdity to your conversations.

    One player draws a question card, and other players respond with their funniest or most outrageous answer card. The results are often a mix of hilarity and unpredictability, making the Chat Against Humanity bot a favorite party game for social gatherings.

    You'll need to add the bot to your group chat, and like the Werewolf bot, players need to open a private chat with the bot to see their cards and choose from them. You can also play a clone of Cards Against Humanity with your friends online.

    5. Chessy

    This inline bot lets you challenge your friends to a game of chess on Telegram. It's convenient for playing chess matches without leaving your group chat. Chessy bot is inline, and you don't need to add it to your chat; you can jump into chess matches within private group chats without needing to leave the conversation.

    To start a game, simply type @chessybot in the group chat and select your side—you can choose to play as either white or black. The bot will display the chessboard as a message right there in the chat. If you prefer better graphics, you can try one of the many apps for chess players available.

    6. Poker

    If you're feeling lucky and love a good poker night, the Poker bot is ready to deal you a hand. You can join random public tables in the bot's private to test your skills against others or host a private table for only your selected friends.

    The Poker bot displays your cards and stats in the bot's private chat and showcases the game table and chips in the group chat. Start the bot and then type @PokerBot in your group chat to start a poker game with your friends. It's time to show off your poker face!

    If you don't like the Telegram bot's version of poker, you can talk with your friends and play games like poker on Discord instead.

    7. Quizarium

    Quizarium is the perfect game bot for those who love trivia. You can only play this game in a group chat with friends. Add the bot to your group chat, select the topic and number of rounds, and get ready to answer questions.

    As you're playing Quizarium bot will provide hints by revealing some letters of the answer. Once the game is over, it announces the winner so that everyone knows who the new trivia king or queen is. The topics you can select are geography, history, music, movies, society, and literature.

    8. Wordi

    Wordi is an engaging word game where you're given a set of letters, and your mission is to form as many words as possible with them. You can challenge your friends or play against random opponents to see who can come up with the most impressive word list.

    Wordi bot is an inline bot, meaning you can challenge your friends without leaving your chat window. Just type @WordiBot in your friend's private chat or a group chat to initiate the game. Tap the available letters to form a word.

    The game will come to an end when all the possible words have been formed, and the player who creates the most words is declared the winner.

    Level Up Your Group Chats With Telegram Game Bots

    With these exciting Telegram game bots, you can turn your group chats into lively gaming arenas and add an extra layer of enjoyment to your conversations. From the strategic challenges of chess to the suspense-filled Werewolf, these bots offer a diverse range of fun experiences for everyone.

    So, what are you waiting for? Add a game bot to your Telegram group chat, grab your friends, and go have some fun!

    FAQ

    Q: Are Bots Safe in Telegram?

    Generally, bots on Telegram are safe. However, you should be cautious as to what data you're sharing with various bots. You should read each bot's terms and conditions and privacy policy to understand how that bot will use your collected user data. Scammers can create malicious bot to steal people's data, so beware of such bots.

    Q: Do Telegram Bots Get Deleted?

    Yes, if a bot is involved in illegal activities, Telegram may delete that bot from its platform. The bot owners can also choose to voluntarily delete their bots. You'll see a message on Telegram when a bot you've joined is removed.

    Q: How to Report a Bot on Telegram?

    You can report a suspicious bot on Telegram using various ways. You can use Telegram's own @notoscam bot to send the company proof of why a bot should be deleted from the platform. Another option is to report your conversation with the suspicious bot using the usual report option. The third way is to email Telegram at abuse@telegram.org with the details about the bot that you think is suspicious.
