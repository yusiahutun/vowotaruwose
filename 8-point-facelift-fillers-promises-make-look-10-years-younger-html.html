    Woman, 47, tries facelift done with FILLERS to take ten years off

    When cheeks and eyes begin to hollow out it can give the face a tired and sagging appearance, with some women choosing to go under the knife. However the 8-Point Facelift has hailed as the ultimate solution to loss of facial volume without the need for surgery.

    04 Mar 2024, 00:00

    When cheeks and eyes begin to hollow out it can give the face a tired and sagging appearance, with some women choosing to go under the knife.

    However the 8-Point Facelift has hailed as the ultimate solution to loss of facial volume without the need for surgery.

    It involves injecting filler into strategic points and promises a better effect in just 30 minutes, for a tenth of the price of the full procedure.

    Milena Georgieva, 47, from West Sussex, tried the 8-Point Facelift after noticing her face had lost a lot of volume (pictured before, left). She was thrilled with the results, which she said made her look younger and fresher (right)

    She says: 'My face has lost a lot of volume and I began to feel very aware and conscious of the ageing process. The 8-Point Facelift seemed like the perfect procedure; restoring volume semi-permanently'

    Milena Georgieva, 47, from West Sussex, decided to undergo the procedure for FEMAIL as she was keen to rejuvenate her face without going under the knife.

    The cost begins at £980 for up to 3ml syringes of dermal filler, and can go up to £1,300 if combined with other anti-aging muscle relaxing treatments for lines and wrinkles, such as Botox in multiple areas.

    The revolutionary procedure gives a more natural result and is said to take ten years off an ageing face. 

    It is ideal in anyone who is starting to notice some skin laxity such as sagging skin, tired under eye area, and jowls along the jawline.

    One treatment promises results for 12-18 months, but touch-ups are required every few months to maintain them.

    This is because gradually the body breaks down the injected Hyaluronic acid-based fillers.

    Fillers are injected into around eight strategic lifting points in the face - the cheek, tear trough, nasolabial folds (smile lines), mouth corners, pre-jowl area, jawline, lower cheek and Marionette lines (running downwards from the corners of the mouth).

    Milena says: 'The injections felt okay, not too bad, the pain was not unbearable'

    Fillers are injected into around eight strategic lifting points in the face - the cheek, tear trough, nasolabial folds, mouth corners, pre-jowl area, jawline, lower cheek and Marionette lines. One treatment will last 12-18 months

    These are a general guideline, but the actual number of areas and amounts of filler used are tailored to individual needs. A personalised consultation is necessary to determine this, and the treatment takes around 30-50 minutes.

    With no scars or tell-tale signs you've had anything done, the procedure is much less invasive and more subtle compared to a surgical facelift. Yet it can make you look more refreshed.

    WHAT HAPPENS AFTERWARDS? 

    There is very minimal or no downtime. We can apply mineral makeup, and you can often go straight back to work.

    Strenuous exercise, going in a spa/sauna or drinking alcohol for the first 24-48 hours should be avoided.

    We do not recommend having this treatment within two weeks of a special event (in case there is bruising), or within one week of an international flight (this can prolong swelling and increase the chance of infection).

    It was originally created by Bazilian Plastic Surgeon Mauricio de Maio. London-based Dr Joney De Souza trained with him and is now one of the only practitioners in Europe to administer the treatment in his Wigmore Street clinic.

    Milena said: 'Over the last ten years my face has lost a lot of volume and I began to feel very aware and conscious of the ageing process.

    'I would never get plastic surgery such as a facelift, but the idea of the 8-Point Facelift appealed to me. It seemed like the perfect procedure; restoring volume semi-permanently.'

    Dr Joney took Milena's medical history and assessed her suitability for the treatment during a full consultation and together they discussed the areas to be treated. Dr Joney then applied a topical anaesthetic to the treatment areas to minimise discomfort.

    'Immediately afterwards my face was a little swollen and some of the treatment areas protruded'

    Describing the procedure, Milena said: 'The injections felt okay, not too bad, the pain was not unbearable. 

    'They felt like a gentle prick like when you have your blood taken rather than an injection into a muscle – which is much more painful. 

    'The areas by my cheekbones and close to the nasolabial folds were the most painful. Dr Joney showed me the gradual results in a mirror to check the progress.

    'Immediately afterwards my face was a little swollen and some of the treatment areas protruded like large bumps under the skin. 

    'Dr Joney explained this was perfectly normal and would subside within a few days to a week, which it did. I also experienced a little numbness on one side of my face but this subsided quickly as well.

    'My face looked ten years younger – but I didn't look as if I'd had anything done. The wrinkles around my face – eyes, cheeks and mouth – decreased in number and depth. My jawline also tightened.'

    Milena says: 'I'm delighted... People are always commenting how fresh I look, but they have no idea why'

    Three months on and Milena's face has stayed looking fresher with wrinkles smoothed

    Three months on and Milena says her face has stayed looking fresher with wrinkles smoothed.

    She said: 'I'm delighted with the long-lasting results. I'm still able to move my face without a frozen or pulled-back look.

    'People around me are always commenting how fresh I look, but they have no idea why.

    'This treatment leaves no obvious signs and looks very natural. It doesn't take your age away in a false way, but rather makes you look like a better, brighter, healthier and happier version of yourself.

    'Additionally, an extra area that Dr Joney fixed was the frown line between my eyebrows, which I'd had since I was 25 and had always bothered me. Now it's all but gone.'

    HOW IT WAS DONE: THE 8-POINT FACELIFT EXPLAINED STEP-BY-STEP

    Dr Joney said: 'The first step was to look at Milena's whole face at rest and at movement to identify areas showing loss of volume.

    'With that in mind, I created a strategy based on the key areas, upper, lower and mid-face.

    'In Milena's case, her mid-face required more attention, followed by lower face, the area around her mouth and the temporal area to provide her with a degree of eyelift.

    'Following the assessment, we discussed how we would target and prioritise, if within one session or more than one session.

    Dr Joney, pictured with Milena, said: 'In Milena's case, her mid-face required more attention, followed by lower face, the area around her mouth and the temporal area to provide her with a degree of eyelift'

    'We decided to do the treatment in one session followed by a review within two weeks to make adjustments according to the results.

    'Immediately after the injections, we could observe the results that we expected but I discussed with the patient that we would get a small degree of swelling, and that only after two weeks would we be able to fully assess the outcomes and make adjustments.

    'In Milena's case, we also combined Botox for forehead and frown lines.

    'I carry out a full individual assessment with each client, looking into skin type, age, areas of need to be addressed, as well as client wishes and realistic expectations.

    'According the patients budget we might divide the treatment into different stages, focusing on the areas of high priority followed by the ones that would give less benefit, but contribute to the overall result. In all cases the face should be seen as a whole and assessed in such a way.'
