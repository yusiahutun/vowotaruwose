    The best The Rolling Stones lyrics about Unrequited love

    22 May 2024, 00:00

    The Rolling Stones, one of the most iconic rock bands of all time, have been captivating audiences for decades with their timeless music and powerful lyrics. Throughout their extensive discography, the theme of unrequited love has often emerged, delving into the complexities of desire, longing, and heartbreak. In this article, we will explore four specific examples from The Rolling Stones' lyrics and songs that beautifully capture the essence of unrequited love.1. "As Tears Go By":One of the early hits by The Rolling Stones, "As Tears Go By" is a poignant ballad that portrays the pain of unreturned love. The lyrics showcase a narrator who yearns for someone who does not reciprocate their feelings. Lines like "It is the evening of the day / I sit and watch the children play / Smiling faces I can see, but not for me" capture the isolating despair that often accompanies unrequited love.2. "Wild Horses":A soulful and introspective track, "Wild Horses" delves into unrequited love's bittersweet nature. The lyrics paint a picture of a love that is strong and all-consuming, yet ultimately unattainable. Specifically, the lines "Wild horses couldn't drag me away / I watched you suffer a dull aching pain" express the narrator's deep love for someone who brings them emotional anguish but remains out of reach.3. "Angie":While "Angie" is often interpreted as a heartbreak song about the end of a relationship, it also alludes to unrequited love. The haunting refrain, "Angie, don't you weep, all your kisses still taste sweet / I hate that sadness in your eyes," showcases the narrator's struggle with unrequited passion for someone who is consumed by their own sorrow, leaving them unable to return the love.4. "Beast of Burden":Stepping into the realm of unrequited love's aftermath, "Beast of Burden" illustrates a narrator who, despite being wounded by a previous relationship, is still willing to be there for their beloved, even if their love remains unreciprocated. The lyrics, "I'll never be your beast of burden / My back is broad, but it's a hurting / All I want for you to make love to me," encapsulate the selflessness and hopelessness that can linger when love is one-sided.Overall, The Rolling Stones have masterfully explored the intricate nuances of unrequited love through their lyrics. From the dejected melancholy of "As Tears Go By" to the resilient devotion of "Beast of Burden," their songs offer a glimpse into the unspoken pain, longing, and desperation that define this complex theme. Through their poetic and relatable storytelling, The Rolling Stones have solidified themselves as iconic troubadours of unrequited love, allowing listeners to find solace in their own unspoken emotions.
