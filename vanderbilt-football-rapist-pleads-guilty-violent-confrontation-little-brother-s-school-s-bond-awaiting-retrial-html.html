    Vanderbilt football 'rapist' pleads guilty to violent confrontation at his little brother's school

    13 Apr 2024, 00:00

    A former Vanderbilt football player awaiting a retrial in a notorious college rape case has pleaded guilty to a violent confrontation with a security guard at his younger brother’s school.

    Brandon Vandenburg, 22, was sentenced to two years of unsupervised probation after admitting to a misdemeanour charge of using offensive words in a public space.

    As part of a plea deal, prosecutors agreed to drop the misdemeanor charge of battery on a school employee after the incident at Palm Desert Middle School in California on February 27, 2014.

    Brandon Vandenburg, 22, was sentenced to two years of unsupervised probation after admitting to a misdemeanour charge after a confrontation with a security guard

    Vandenburg, who was not present for the hearing on Friday at Larson Justice Center in Indio, must pay a $650 fine and at least $275 in restitution, according to The Tennessean.

    The newspaper reported that he had gone to pick up his two younger brothers when security guard Jaime Tarascio told Vandenburg that he had confiscated a basketball from one of the boys.

    Tarascio reportedly told him that he would only return the ball to a parent or an adult named on the student’s emergency contact form.

    But according to court records, when Tarascio said he would have to confirm Vandenburg’s claim that he was listed on the form, the former athlete allegedly grabbed the guard’s sunglasses – leaving a small scar on his face.

    Vandeburg (left) and Corey Batey (second from right) in a court hearing in June last year. Both had their convictions thrown out after it emerged a juror had failed to disclose that he was a victim of statutory rape

    At the time of the incident, Vandenburg had been free on bail after being indicted along with three others in a rape case at Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tennesse

    According to a report by a sheriff’s deputy, Vandenburg - who is 6’6”and 260 pounds - refused to return the glasses and needed to be forced off the school’s campus.

    He put the glasses down after being told that police had been called.

    Vandenburg’s lawyer later claimed that it was Tarascio who had assaulted his client.

    At the time of the incident, Vandenburg had been free on bail after being indicted along with three others in a rape case at Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tennesse.

    He and Corey Batey were found guilty in January last year for raping an unconscious female student in a dorm room in June 2013 and videotaping the assault using their cellphones.

    Jurors were shown university surveillance video and the players' own graphic cellphone images that put them at the scene.

    Vandenburg has been free on a $400,000 bond since June last year. A retrial is scheduled for later this year

    But their convictions were thrown out and a mistrial was declared after it emerged that the jury foreman had failed to disclose that he was a victim of statutory rape.

    The two remaining defendants alleged to have joined Batey and Vandenburg in the gang rape, former players Jaborian McKenzie and Brandon Banks, cooperated with authorities and have yet to face trial.

    Vandenburg has been free on a $400,000 bond since June last year after prosecutors cited the school incident when asking for his bail to be increased from $350,000.

    The case it set to be retried in March or April this year.
