    Miranda Devine is a prominent Australian columnist, political writer, and commentator. Formerly, she printed her column two times a week in Fairfax Media newspapers, The Sydney Morning Herald and The Sun-Herald, as of now appears in the News Limited newspapers Sunday Telegraph, Perth’s Sunday Times, Daily Telegraph, and Melbourne’s Sunday Herald Sun.

    Early Life

    Miranda was born in the month of July, in 1961 in New York City, United States of America. She is the eldest daughter of her parents, Frank Devine and Jacqueline Magee. Her father was a New Zealand born Australian newspaper editor and journalist, his unfortunate death took place in 2009. She studied at Loreto Kirribilli, an independent Roman Catholic comprehensive single-sex primary and secondary day school for girls, situated in Kirribilli, Australia, and the International School of the Sacred Heart, Tokyo.

    Miranda earned a Masters’s degree in Journalism from Northwestern University, the USA, and a Bachelor of Science degree in Mathematics from Macquarie University, Australia. She attended first-year of architecture at Sydney University and also worked at CSIRO (Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation), a Federal government agency for scientific research in Australia, and worked in the division of Textile Physics for four years.

    Miranda Devine Career

    Earlier, Devine used to work as a reporter and a feature writer for the famous Boston Herald, an American daily newspaper. After coming back to Australia she decided to live in Sydney. During the time of 1989, she joined “The Daily Telegraph” and was employed as an assistant editor, police reporter, and columnist altogether. Previously, she had also worked at the British tabloid newspaper “The Sun” and British newspaper “Sunday Times” in London. Most of her recent columns are focused on United States politics and are published by the New York Post. She has also made appearances on some local media outlets with an objective to promote her articles. She even used to host a show entitled with her own name as “The Miranda Devine Show” on the 2G radio station in Sydney.
