    Singer Calum Scott once had one his own hit songs come on during a dinner date with a potential suitor he'd met on a dating app.

    The Britain's Got Talent alum, 34, who is an openly gay man, recalled the hilarious moment on Nova's Fitzy & Wippa radio show this week.

    He was asked by the hosts to share the 'strangest place' he had ever heard his music play.

    Awkward! Singer Calum Scott once had one his own hit songs come on during a dinner date with a potential suitor he'd met on a dating app; Calum seen in 2017

    'There was one time where I was on a date... I'd arranged the date on Tinder. And I'd gone up to get my table reservation… and this guy didn't know who I was,' Calum explained.

    One of hits began playing over the speakers and Calum was 'just like praying' that his date wouldn't catch on.

    Talent: Calum became a household name in 2015 while competing on Britain's Got Talent; seen in 2017

    Calum became a household name in 2015 while competing on Britain's Got Talent.

    Despite placing sixth in the competition, the UK native became a chart-topper after releasing his cover of pop songstress Robyn's track Dancing On My Own in 2016.

    The single had a resurgence this year by becoming the 'unofficial playoff anthem' for the Philadelphia Phillies baseball team.

    As reported by Billboard in October, Dancing On My Own 'exploded in sales, from a negligible daily amount to nearly 1,100 combined copies across Saturday and Sunday – enough for the song to appear on iTunes’ real time sales Top 100.'

    Bridges: His highly-anticipated second album, Bridges, was released in June

     Tour life: To support the album, he kicked off the Bridges World Tour in North America in July

    Calum signed a record deal with Capitol in 2017 and his debut album, Only Human, was released the following year. It featured the single You Are The Reason.

    He released several EPs in early 2021 before premiering the first single off his highly-anticipated second album, Bridges, that June.

    He released two more songs from the album before revealing that Bridges would be available to fans in June 2022.

    To support the album, he kicked off the Bridges World Tour in North America in July.

    He is currently performing sold-out shows in Australia.
