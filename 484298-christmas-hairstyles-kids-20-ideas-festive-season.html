    Tell your child they are getting a new haircut or hairstyle, and watch the excitement. Children are happy and excited when they get their hair done, and you will be surprised that they also have an idea of what they want. Of course, not the mature style you are used to, but one with a fun vibe. So, what kids Christmas hairstyles are the best?

    1. Half-up half-down ponytail

    The half-up and half-down ponytail is one of the best Christmas hairstyles for little girls. It is a natural look that is easy to style, meaning your child will spend less time at the salon. So, how do you achieve this look?

    Create longer curls and split them into horizontal sections to create beautiful half-up and half-down ponytails.

    2. Banded half updo

    One thing about kids is that you can play around with their hair as it is rich and full of life. And nothing brings the Christmas spirit like the blended half-updo. However, it is a semi-advanced look that requires mini elastic, a sectioning comb and kid-friendly edge control. If you cannot make it work, you can always go to the stylist to do it.

    You must determine the number of vertical sections you deem fit for your kid. After creating the vertical sections, create small ponytails and pull them vertically into the next.

    3. Sleeky baby afro

    If you want something cute, the sleeky baby afro is the one. One of the best ways of adding a sleek touch to the baby afro is adding a slight side part and smoothening down the hairline horizontally with a brush. You can use a baby-safe edge control or gel to make it look outstanding.

    4. Rainbow box braids

    You know that kids love colours, and they can never get enough. So, one of the best ways of fulfilling this desire is to let them go for rainbow box braids. The multiple colours of the rainbow look fantastic, and your child will be the talk of the town at that Christmas gathering they have been looking forward to all year.

    The standard colours to incorporate here include pink, blue, orange, and green. These colours have a unique allure that is hard to achieve with other colour combinations. They will display a gorgeous colour spectrum.

    5. Voluminous afro

    If your kid has natural hair, there's no need to hide. Instead, try the voluminous afro that looks perfect and brings out that laid-back feel. The good thing with afros is that they are versatile and can be styled however you desire.

    The fluff idea is excellent as it adds additional volume. Also, it is one of the most suitable Christmas hairstyles for 10 year olds as it doesn't put stress on the roots.

    6. Heart-shaped braided ponytail

    Christmas hairstyles for kids don't get better than this. The classic heart-shaped braided ponytail is something your kid will look forward to once they spot the photo. Not only does it perfectly fit the festivities, but it is suitable for weddings and other special events.

    A classic heart-shaped ponytail starts by dividing the hair down the middle into two sections. Then, create another part along one side, starting at the crown and curving the heart toward the ear. Next, clip near the ear and begin a Dutch braid using the hair from outside the heart part. Repeat the entire process until you achieve your look.

    7. Half-up braided bun with charmed braids

    One thing about kids' hairstyles is that they can play around with the most bizarre of looks, but it wouldn't look off to them. You can always try anything you want on your child, as it is all about bringing about a playful vibe.

    The half-up braided bun with charmed braids brings you these elements perfectly. The look adds a stylish twist with two cornrows near the ears embellished in gold cuffs and beads.

    8. Reindeer antler buns

    How fun is this hairstyle? The reindeer antler bun is a fabulous hairdo for children. The braids are adorable, and the space bun idea is out of this world. The look works well for younger children or kids with short hair.

    9. The fade

    The fade is famous for boys and ladies who want short hair, so why not for your kid? The fade is a cut with shorter sides, like an undercut, but with a gradual increase in length.

    When your kid visits the barbershop, inform them whether you want a high, regular, or low fade. These variations will give precise results.

    10. Cornrows with beads

    Children love showing off their unique styles; adding cool accessories like beads is the best way to do this. Beads have always been a creative way of adding glam to braids, and children love it. However, make sure the beads are colourful to bring out that remarkable, classic, and vibrant look.

    11. Braided top bun with natural hair

    The braided top bun with natural hair is one of the best Christmas hairstyles for toddlers. This incredible hairstyle combines natural hair with a braided top bun. Of course, the bun looks super cool and stands out. The sections are loose, meaning that the hairstyle is comfortable. If you want this to last long, keep the braided and unbraided areas moisturized.

    12. Box braids ponytail

    Box braids are perfect for older kids and teenagers. You wouldn't want to have this on your 5-year-old as they wouldn't have the patience to wait for the braids to be put in. However, they can wait it out when they are a bit older. A fantastic thing about box braids is that they can last for four to six weeks if well-maintained.

    13. Braids with elastic bands

    There is nothing as extraordinary for kids as coloured elastic bands. Such styles have been popular for years as the elastic bands add fun to any look. And the most exciting thing is that you can add elastic bands to all braided hairstyles.

    14. Fulani-inspired braids

    Fulani-inspired braids are the best way of getting in touch with the African vibe. They are one of the most-loved tribal braids as they are unique and eye-catching. Ascertain that you keep the inside of the braids looser and smaller than you would with an adult to make it comfortable for the child.

    15. Silk ponytail

    Another fantastic way of styling your kid's hair is using silk. Divide the hair in half and fishtail braid each one. Then, pull the strand forward, and secure it with a bobby for the perfect crown effects. You can add a flower or anything else for that extra touch.

    16. Baby afro with flowers

    A basic baby afro looks boring, but flowers make it more vibrant. Do not complicate your child's style by going for adult styles that make their experience boring, but add lovely flowers.

    17. Side cornrow ponytail for natural hair

    If you don't want your kid to add anything else to their hair, you can go for the side cornrow ponytail for natural hair. You can start with cornrows and braid them halfway towards the far side of the head. Then, gather them into a side ponytail for the ultimate look.

    18. Pigtail braids

    The pigtail look is one of the most loved by children. One thing about the style is that it is fast to install and less stressful on the roots. You must first part your child's hair in half and then half in two.

    Once you have down this, braid each part you've sectioned (a simple braid will do). Then, tighten it with invisible hair braids and add cute accessories.

    19. Half-moon part and high fade

    The half-moon part with a high fade is an incredible variation of the buzz fade. The significant difference and what makes this style unique is the half-moon.

    The high top is a classic haircut for boys. The rounded top looks incredible as it doesn't give the volume of an afro but still looks like one. It is a subtle way of telling people you love the afro but still fancy short hair.
