    Jesse L. Martin's biography

    Jesse Lemont Martin was born in Rocky Mount, Virginia, United States. He is the third of four sons, and his parents are Jesse Reed Watkins (father) and Virginia Price (mother). Unlike most children, Jesse was born two months premature.

    Jesse L. Martin's parents divorced when he was two years old. As a result, his mother remarried and moved to Buffalo, New York. Ultimately, Jesse and his siblings adopted their stepfather's surname, Martin, instead of their father's surname, Watkins.

    In the fourth grade, Jesse joined an after-school drama programme. It was his first time speaking in front of people, and he was shy because he had a thick Southern accent. Jesse disliked speaking in an accent, but his teacher thought it would be helpful for him. He then played a character that made him emerge from his shell.

    Where did Jesse L. Martin go to high school?

    Concerning Jesse L. Martin's education, he went to high school at the Buffalo Academy for Visual and Performing Arts. After graduating from high school, he enrolled in New York University and did a theatre programme at the Tisch School of the Arts.

    Martin then toured for almost a year as a John Houseman's Acting Company member and worked regionally at Hartford Stage, Arena Stage, Cleveland Playhouse, and Louisville's Actors Theatre.

    Jesse made his professional debut in theatres in 1993. He portrayed Tom Collins in Rent, a Broadway musical. In 1995, Martin debuted as a TV actor playing Quincy in One Life to Live.
